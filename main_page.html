Welcome to F1 Cars

Formula 1 is the highest class of international racing for single-seater cars. With top speeds exceeding 350 km/h and state-of-the-art engineering, these cars represent the peak of motorsport technology and innovation.

About F1 Cars & Engines

F1 cars are built for speed, aerodynamics, and efficiency. Every part of an F1 car is designed to maximize performance on the track. The body is made from carbon fiber for strength and light weight. Wings and diffusers generate downforce to keep the car glued to the track at high speeds.

The heart of an F1 car is its hybrid power unit. Since 2014, F1 has used 1.6-liter V6 turbocharged engines combined with electric components:

  • Internal Combustion Engine (ICE): A small but powerful 1.6L V6 turbo engine producing over 700 horsepower.
  • MGU-K (Motor Generator Unit - Kinetic): Recovers energy from braking and stores it in batteries.
  • MGU-H (Motor Generator Unit - Heat): Harvests energy from exhaust heat and improves turbo efficiency.
  • Energy Store (Battery): Holds electrical energy to be deployed for additional power.
  • Turbocharger: Increases engine power by compressing air into the combustion chamber.

Combined, these systems produce over 1,000 horsepower while being highly fuel-efficient — a key challenge in modern F1 engineering.
